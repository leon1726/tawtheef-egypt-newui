FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common career questions from Egyptian professionals.

How long should my CV be for jobs in Egypt?
For most roles in Egypt, a one-to-two-page CV is standard. Fresh graduates and those with under five years of experience should aim for one page. Candidates with ten or more years of progressive experience may extend to two pages. Avoid padding — every line should justify its space. Recruiters typically spend 7–10 seconds on an initial scan, so density and clarity matter more than length.
Should I include a photo on my CV when applying for jobs in Egypt?
In Egypt, including a professional photo is common and generally expected for local companies, particularly in sales, customer-facing, and corporate roles. For international companies or technical roles, a photo is optional. If you include one, use a recent, high-resolution headshot with a neutral background and professional attire.
What is the best way to answer "What is your expected salary?" in an interview?
Early in the process, try to redirect: "I'd like to learn more about the full scope of responsibilities before settling on a number — what is the budgeted range for this role?" If pressed, provide a researched range anchored slightly above your target. Never state a figure lower than your actual minimum.
How do I explain a gap in my employment history?
Address gaps directly and briefly, then pivot to what you did during that time. Employers respect honesty far more than evasion. If the gap involved family responsibilities, freelance work, education, or health — say so clearly. A confident, matter-of-fact explanation almost always satisfies recruiters.
Is it worth getting international certifications like PMP, CPA, or AWS in Egypt?
Yes, significantly so. PMP holders command a measurable salary premium. CPA and CMA certifications are highly regarded in finance. AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure certifications open doors to both local tech companies and international remote opportunities. These credentials signal commitment, rigor, and internationally benchmarked expertise.
How long does a typical hiring process take in Egypt?
Startups often move in one to two weeks. Mid-size local companies typically take two to four weeks. Multinational corporations often run processes of four to eight weeks. Following up professionally after each stage is appropriate — silence is rarely a rejection in Egyptian hiring culture.
How can I transition into tech with no prior technical experience?
Identify bridge roles that combine your existing expertise with technology — such as product management, technical sales, business analysis, or UX research. Pursue structured learning through bootcamps or platforms like Coursera and Udemy. Build a portfolio of small projects and join Egypt's active tech communities on LinkedIn and GitHub.
Is a cover letter required when applying for jobs in Egypt?
Cover letters are not universally required, but submitting one when others don't is a meaningful differentiator. A strong cover letter addresses why you want this specific role at this specific company and demonstrates relevant experience. Generic template cover letters are worse than none.
What are the most in-demand skills in Egypt's job market right now?
The highest-demand skills currently include software development (Python, JavaScript, React, Node.js), data analysis and SQL, digital marketing, English proficiency at B2 or above, project management, cloud computing, and UI/UX design. Bilingual professionals — Arabic, English, and French — are particularly valued in customer-facing and multinational roles.
How do I handle a job offer that is lower than expected?
Never accept or decline on the spot. Express genuine enthusiasm and ask for 24–48 hours to review the full offer. Evaluate total compensation — benefits, bonuses, flexibility, and growth trajectory all have real value. If the base is lower than expected, counteroffer with a specific number backed by market data: "Based on my research for this level in Egypt, I was targeting X — is there flexibility?"
