FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common career questions from Egyptian professionals.

How long should my CV be for jobs in Egypt?
For most roles in Egypt, a one-to-two-page CV is standard. Fresh graduates and those with under five years of experience should aim for one page. Candidates with ten or more years of progressive experience in senior or specialized roles may extend to two pages. Avoid padding — every line should justify its space. Recruiters typically spend 7–10 seconds on an initial CV scan, so density and clarity matter more than length.
Should I include a photo on my CV when applying for jobs in Egypt?
In Egypt, including a professional photo is common and generally expected for local companies, particularly in sales, customer-facing, and corporate roles. However, for international companies or roles explicitly focused on technical skills, a photo is optional. If you include one, use a recent, high-resolution headshot with a neutral background and professional attire. Avoid casual selfies or informal settings.
What is the best way to answer "What is your expected salary?" in an interview?
The ideal approach depends on the interview stage. Early in the process, try to redirect: "I'd like to learn more about the full scope of responsibilities before settling on a number — what is the budgeted range for this role?" If pressed, provide a researched range rather than a single figure, anchored slightly above your target. For example: "Based on my research and experience level, I'm targeting between 15,000 and 18,000 EGP, though I'm open to discussing the full compensation package." Never state a figure lower than your actual minimum.
How do I explain a gap in my employment history?
Address gaps directly and briefly, then pivot to what you did during that time and what you learned. Employers respect honesty far more than evasion. If the gap involved family responsibilities, freelance work, education, or health — say so clearly. If it was a period of job searching, acknowledge it and emphasize how you stayed current: courses taken, skills developed, or projects completed. The worst response is to avoid the topic or fabricate employment dates. A confident, matter-of-fact explanation almost always satisfies recruiters.
Is it worth getting international certifications like PMP, CPA, or AWS in Egypt?
Yes, significantly so. International certifications are among the highest-ROI career investments available to Egyptian professionals. PMP holders command a measurable salary premium in project management roles. CPA and CMA certifications are highly regarded in finance. AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure certifications open doors to both local tech companies and international remote opportunities. These credentials signal commitment, rigor, and internationally benchmarked expertise — all of which differentiate candidates in a competitive market.
How long does a typical hiring process take in Egypt?
Hiring timelines in Egypt vary considerably. Startups and small companies often move in one to two weeks. Mid-size local companies typically take two to four weeks. Multinational corporations and large enterprises often run processes of four to eight weeks, particularly for senior or specialized roles. Government-adjacent organizations can take months. Following up professionally after each interview stage is appropriate and generally well-received — silence is rarely a rejection in Egyptian hiring culture.
How can I transition into tech with no prior technical experience?
A technical career pivot is very achievable with focused effort. Identify bridge roles that combine your existing expertise with technology — such as product management, technical sales, business analysis, or UX research. These roles value domain knowledge alongside technical understanding. Pursue structured learning through bootcamps, platforms like Coursera and Udemy, or self-directed study. Build a portfolio of small projects, even unpaid or personal ones. Join Egypt's active tech communities on LinkedIn, GitHub, and local Slack groups to accelerate your network.
Is a cover letter required when applying for jobs in Egypt?
Cover letters are not universally required in the Egyptian market, but submitting one when others don't is a meaningful differentiator. When a position explicitly requests one, treat it as a serious part of your application — not a formality. A strong cover letter addresses why you want this specific role at this specific company, demonstrates relevant experience, and shows you've researched the organization. Generic, template cover letters are worse than none. If the role doesn't request one, a brief, professional introductory message in the email body can achieve a similar effect.
What are the most in-demand skills in Egypt's job market right now?
Based on current job market data, the highest-demand skills in Egypt include software development (Python, JavaScript, React, Node.js), data analysis and SQL, digital marketing (performance marketing, SEO, content), English proficiency at B2 or above, project management (PMP, Agile), cloud computing (AWS, Azure), and UI/UX design. Bilingual or trilingual professionals — Arabic, English, and French — are particularly valued in customer-facing, BPO, and multinational roles. AI literacy is increasingly mentioned in job descriptions across sectors.
How do I handle a job offer that is lower than expected?
Never accept or decline on the spot. Express genuine enthusiasm for the role and ask for time to review the full offer — 24 to 48 hours is standard and professionally appropriate. Evaluate total compensation, not just base salary: benefits, bonuses, commissions, flexibility, training budget, and growth trajectory all have real value. If the base is lower than expected, counteroffer with a specific number backed by market data and your experience. Framing matters: "Based on my research for this level in Egypt, I was targeting X — is there flexibility to get closer to that?" works better than "Your offer is too low."
